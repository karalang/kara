# Brainstorming v24 — Trait effect declarations: unresolved edges

**Status: RESOLVED.** All questions answered; design.md updated; implementation work tracked separately (see Next steps at bottom).

v23 resolved the `public_effects = "declared" | "inferred"` knob for plain `pub fn`. During implementation survey for v23, two trait-related discoveries forced this follow-up doc:

1. The existing `docs/design.md` § Trait Coherence and Effects already specifies the open-world rule for public traits (declare) and the closed-world rule for private traits (infer from union of visible impls). Our initial v23 Q4 resolution — "always declared regardless of visibility" — contradicted this and was reverted.
2. The current `src/effectchecker.rs` does not handle trait declarations at all. Traits exist in the AST but are silently skipped by the effect checker. Any trait-effect checking is unimplemented.

This doc resolves the remaining specification edges before implementation can proceed. None of these questions block v23's `public_effects` knob from shipping; they block trait effect *checking* from being built soundly.

---

## Q1. Does `public_effects = "inferred"` relax the public-trait-declares rule? — RESOLVED

**Resolution — no.** The open-world soundness argument in § Trait Coherence and Effects is independent of the `public_effects` knob. A downstream crate impl-ing a public trait must see a fixed ceiling at the trait declaration; otherwise generic callers leak effects across crate boundaries when new impls are published. `public_effects` governs plain `pub fn` effect declarations only. Public traits always declare per § Trait Coherence and Effects, regardless of `public_effects`. Private traits still infer per that section.

Already recorded in design.md § Effect Inference and Boundaries ("Trait method effect policy is specified separately... and is not relaxed by `public_effects = "inferred"`"). No further design.md edit needed.

---

## Q2. Trait-level `with ...` shorthand — is it a ceiling, a shorthand, or both? — RESOLVED

**Resolution — (b) shorthand / default, with method-level *replacing* trait-level when both are present.** A `with` clause on the trait header applies to every method that omits its own `with`. A method's own `with` clause fully replaces the trait-level default for that method — no unioning with the trait-level set.

Rationale: method-level `with` is the authoritative declaration. Trait-level exists purely to reduce stutter when a trait's methods share a common envelope. Unioning would hide the authoritative declaration behind implicit context; replacement keeps each method's ceiling readable from its own signature alone.

Recorded in design.md § Trait Coherence and Effects ("Trait-level `with` clauses are defaults for methods that declare no `with` of their own.").

---

## Q3. Supertrait effect inheritance — RESOLVED

Given `trait Foo: Bar`, does `Foo` inherit `Bar`'s method-level effect ceilings?

**Background.** At the time v24 was drafted, design.md did not specify supertraits at all. The feature was surveyed and resolved as part of this thread — see design.md § Trait Constraints (Supertraits). Supertraits are now in the language with the narrow charter "logical constraint shorthand, not class extension": `trait Foo: Bar` is exactly `where Self: Bar`, no data inheritance, no method body reuse, no subtype substitutability.

**Resolution — (a) Independent.** Each trait's effect ceilings apply at its own impl block. `trait Writer: Reader` does not transfer `Reader`'s ceilings onto `Writer`'s methods; an `impl Writer for T` is checked against `Writer`'s ceilings, and the separately-required `impl Reader for T` is checked against `Reader`'s. This is consistent with the "logical constraint only" framing: the supertrait relation guarantees both impls exist, and each is verified against its own trait's rules. Nothing propagates across.

Recorded in design.md § Trait Constraints (Supertraits) under "Constraints and effects."

## Q4. Diagnostic shape for impl-exceeds-trait — RESOLVED

**Resolution:** reuse the declared-vs-inferred diagnostic format used by public-fn effect verification (`src/effectchecker.rs.verify_declarations`).

- **Error code:** reuse the existing declared-vs-inferred code, with an additional trait-method context field to distinguish impl-vs-trait from pub-fn verification.
- **Primary span:** the impl method signature.
- **Secondary/note span:** the trait method declaration that set the ceiling.
- **Suggested fix:** propose *narrowing* the impl (removing or refactoring the offending call). Do not auto-suggest widening the trait ceiling — the ceiling is load-bearing for every other impl and for every generic caller, so widening must be a deliberate choice by the trait author, not an auto-fix the compiler offers on behalf of one impl.

Implementation-level only — no design.md edit needed. Referenced from the v24 implementation checklist below.

## Q5. Default method body checking — RESOLVED

**Resolution — confirmed.** A default method body's inferred effects must form a subset of the trait method's declared ceiling (trait-level shorthand expanded per Q2's resolution). Same subset rule as impl bodies. The trait author cannot smuggle in effects that the method's signature does not declare.

Recorded in design.md § Trait Coherence and Effects ("Default method bodies are checked against the method's declared ceiling.").

---

## Q6. Effects on trait associated functions — RESOLVED

**Resolution — confirmed.** Associated functions (trait items without `self`) are checked identically to methods: per-item ceiling, trait-level shorthand, default-body check, and impl-site subset rule all apply unchanged.

Recorded in design.md § Trait Coherence and Effects ("Associated functions (trait items without `self`) are checked identically to methods.").

---

## Q7. Private trait inference precision — RESOLVED

**Resolution — both parts confirmed.**

- **SCC-aware inference.** Private trait method ceilings are nodes in the same strongly-connected-component graph as private `fn` effect inference. Impl bodies that call through the trait, and default method bodies that recurse into impls, resolve simultaneously — a single fixed-point per method.
- **Unimpled private trait = unbound ceiling.** An unimpled private trait is effect-opaque, not effect-empty. Callers through it propagate `with _` (same rule as a pub trait with no declared ceiling). This is the forgiving choice: work-in-progress traits do not produce surprise errors at every call site until the first impl lands.

Recorded in design.md § Trait Coherence and Effects ("Private-trait inference is SCC-aware." and "A private trait with no visible impls has an unbound ceiling.").
